About Us

The OntoChoice project is a collaborative research initiative dedicated to developing systematic methodologies for selecting, evaluating, and reusing ontological terms and vocabularies across research domains. The team is actively developing a comprehensive document that explores critical challenges in ontology selection, with a multifaceted approach focusing on several key areas: cataloging existing evaluation techniques, identifying use cases, developing potential tooling, and creating recommendations for ontology sustainability and community adoption. Their work addresses complex challenges such as term context, reuse strategies, and interoperability, with particular attention to emerging technologies like large language models.
